Education isn’t just about learning facts and figures—it’s about empowerment, transformation, and building a future where knowledge becomes a tool for freedom. For millions of students, Ahmed Abo Zaid, the creator of “Droos Online,” was the embodiment of that belief. His unique approach to teaching didn’t just make subjects easier to understand; it made students believe in their potential.

Recently, Ahmed was detained by the Egyptian government, and this event raises questions that affect us all. What happens when voices that seek to uplift are silenced? How can we, as individuals and a community, ensure that education and free expression remain untouchable rights for everyone? These are questions I want to explore with you today.

A Teacher Who Taught Beyond the Classroom

Ahmed Abo Zaid wasn’t just a teacher; he was a revolutionary in the field of education. His lessons, available for free through “Droos Online,” reached millions of students across the Arab world, breaking barriers of access and cost. For many, he was the difference between giving up on a subject and mastering it. He turned learning into an engaging journey that was accessible to anyone with an internet connection.

I personally gained so much from his teachings. When I struggled with concepts that felt impossible to grasp, his videos were like a light in the dark. Ahmed had this incredible way of making even the most complex ideas feel approachable and achievable. And I know I’m not alone in saying this—thousands, maybe even millions, of students feel the same.

What His Detention Means for Us

Ahmed’s detention isn’t just about one man—it’s about the larger struggle for freedom of expression and the right to education. His work represents the transformative power of knowledge, and his detention reminds us of how vital it is to protect those who strive to make education accessible to all.

This isn’t a story to simply read and move on from. This is a moment to reflect and engage. Ahmed’s work inspired a generation of learners, and his situation challenges us to ask: How can we ensure that education remains a right, not a privilege? How do we support individuals like Ahmed who dedicate their lives to empowering others?

What Can We Do?

As readers and as members of a global community, we have the power to keep Ahmed’s mission alive. Here are some ways we can act:

  • Spread Awareness: Share Ahmed’s story and the impact of “Droos Online.” Let people know about the incredible work he’s done and the challenges he’s facing.
  • Support Accessible Education: If you’ve benefited from Ahmed’s teachings, consider mentoring someone else, sharing resources, or contributing to initiatives that promote free education.
  • Stay Informed: Keep an eye on developments related to his situation and others like it. Knowledge is the first step to action.

A Call to Action

Ahmed Abo Zaid’s story is still being written, and we have a role to play in it. By standing for the values he represents—free and accessible education, perseverance, and the courage to teach against all odds—we ensure that his impact doesn’t fade, regardless of the challenges he faces.

This blog isn’t just about reflecting on what Ahmed has done for us—it’s about stepping up and continuing his mission. Let’s ensure that the next generation has the same opportunities Ahmed gave to us, and let’s remind the world of the power of one educator to change countless lives.

How has Ahmed Abo Zaid impacted your life? Share your thoughts in the comments or on social media. Let’s come together to celebrate his work and amplify his legacy.
